<volume level="100"/><voice required="Name=IVONA 2 Brian OEM"><rate absspeed="-4">Secondly, the other fountain from which experience furnisheth the understanding with ideas is, the perception of the operations of our own mind within us, as it is employed about the ideas it has got; which operations, when the soul comes to reflect on and consider, do furnish the understanding with another set of ideas, which could not be had from things without.<silence msec="500"/></rate></voice> 
Antra, kitas šaltinis iš kurio patirtis aprūpina supratimą su idėjomis yra, suvokimas operacijų mūsų nuosavo intelekto viduje mūsų, kaip jis yra pritaikytas apie idėjas jis yra gavęs; kurios operacijos, kada siela ateina reflektuoti ant ir nagrinėti, daro aprūpina supratimą su kitokiu rinkiniu idėjų, kuriuos negali būti turėjo iš daiktų išorės.

<volume level="100"/><voice required="Name=IVONA 2 Brian OEM"><rate absspeed="-4">And such are perception, thinking, doubting, believing, reasoning, knowing, willing, and all the different actings of our own minds; which we being conscious of, and observing in ourselves, do from these receive into our understandings as distinct ideas as we do from bodies affecting our senses.<silence msec="500"/></rate></voice>
Ir toks yra suvokimas, mastymas, abejojimas, tikėjimas, samprotavimas, žinojimas, norėjimas, ir visi skirtingi veikimai mūsų nuosavų intelektų; kuriuos mes būdami samoningi apie, ir stebėdami savyje, darome iš šių gauname į mūsų supratimus kaip skirtingas idėjas kaip mes darome iš kūnų veikiančių mūsų jutimus.

<volume level="100"/><voice required="Name=IVONA 2 Brian OEM"><rate absspeed="-4">This source of ideas every man has wholly in himself; and though it be not sense, as having nothing to do with external objects, yet it is very like it, and might properly enough be called internal sense.<silence msec="500"/></rate></voice>

<volume level="100"/><voice required="Name=IVONA 2 Brian OEM"><rate absspeed="-4">But as I call the other SENSATION, so I Call this REFLECTION, the ideas it affords being such only as the mind gets by reflecting on its own operations within itself.<silence msec="500"/></rate></voice>

<volume level="100"/><voice required="Name=IVONA 2 Brian OEM"><rate absspeed="-4">By reflection then, in the following part of this discourse, I would be understood to mean, that notice which the mind takes of its own operations, and the manner of them, by reason whereof there come to be ideas of these operations in the understanding.<silence msec="500"/></rate></voice>
